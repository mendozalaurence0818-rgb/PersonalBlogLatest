Yoyenk's Life Notes

Childhood Story

1I went to three different schools when I was a kid. 2I studied preschool in a Barangay Hall. 3I transferred to General M. Hizon Elementary School. 4After that, I studied Grade 1 and Grade 2 at Manila Cathedral School. 5Later on, I moved again and finished Grade 3 to Grade 6 at Holy Heart Christian Academy. 6I was a normal student who tried my best every day. 7Medyo nahihirapan akong mag focus sa lessons minsan. 8Pero kahit ganon, okay pa rin ang mga grades ko. 9I would describe myself as a mid-tier student who just wanted to pass and learn. 10I did not go outside a lot when I was young. 11Since we lived in a building, I spent most of my time inside our unit. 12I became very close to my computer because it was my main source of fun. 13Mahilig talaga ako maglaro, manood, at mag-explore sa PC. 14One big memory I have is when my foot got stuck in a bike wheel. 15That accident left a scar that I still see today. 16It hurt a lot. I did not have a phone during my childhood years. 17I only got my first phone when I reached Grade 7. 18My childhood was simple but full of small stories. 19Even though I stayed indoors most of the time, I still learned many things. 20Looking back, those years helped shape who I am now.

Teenage Years

1Most of my teenage years were spent at home because COVID-19 hit hard. 2Everything suddenly went online and life felt very different. 3Before the pandemic, my Grade 7 and Grade 8 years were still face-to-face. 4I had my first girlfriend when I was fifteen. 5I was still a teen, but I learned a lot from that relationship. 6We broke up in the middle of the pandemic. 7After that, I started focusing on MLBB to distract myself. 8I also slowly began my editing career during this time. 9My MLBB journey started strong and exciting. 10My friends and I created our own team and organization, we grew fast and joined many tournaments. 11Eventually, our team started to fade because people became busy. 12I then thought of hosting tournaments that were free to join but had prizes up to one thousand pesos. I became the main person in our group. 13I was the editor and the streamer for every tournament game. 14I also handled the format and flow of each event. 15Later on,I got my first official job as a Graphics Designer at Rei Esports.16The pay was good and the experience was even better.17During this time,I worked for Z4 Esports, which was handled by Zapnu. 18I also worked with Blackdime PH, ONIC PH, and Blacklist International. 19I even became the official Graphics Designer for Sensui, Coach Jeff, and Coach Bam. 20I eventually had to quit because face-to-face classes were coming back during my SHS years.

College Experience

1My first month in college was exciting because I met a group of fifteen friends. 2Over time, our group slowly got smaller. 3Now, only five of us remain and we are still close. 4I chose BSIT because I first tried HTML back in JHS and SHS. 5We coded using Notepad, and I found it very interesting. 6I also learned that IT was in demand, which made me more curious. 7Even though coding is hard for me, I still want to improve. 8I sometimes struggle to listen during lessons. 9Because of that, learning feels slow but still possible. 10I am not very good at coding yet, but I am trying. 11Every semester teaches me something new. 12There are days when I feel stressed, but I keep going. 13My main goal is to graduate and become better. 14I want to build skills that will help me in the future. 15College has pushed me to be more responsible. 16I also decided to join an organization to grow more. 17Joining UE Zenith Esports turned out to be a great choice. 18I met new people who motivated me. 19I worked hard to prove myself inside the org. Now, I am the EVP for Media Communications in UE Zenith Esports. 20This role helps me grow and gives me confidence for my future career.
